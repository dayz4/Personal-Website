~ About Me ~


Computer Science

I'm currently a senior studying computer science at Cornell University with a focus in graphics and a minor in biology, set to graduate May 2018.

My interests lie primarily in the visual side of computer science. My fascination with animation and game design was what originally led me down this path, and while the dream of breaking into those industries remains strong to this day, I have also discovered a keen interest in other aspects of visual computing, such as graphics, computer vision, data visualizations, and image analysis.

I also find biology to be, in a word, amazing. It's a rapidly evolving field that is constantly discovering things that could change the world (for better or for worse), and now more than ever, technology plays a crucial role in making these discoveries possible. To stand at the intersection of computer science and biology would be incredible.

It's crazy sometimes to look back on the things that we, as a society, have accomplished through computer science, and I look forward to continuing to push that boundary that separates the impossible from the possible. Check out my Projects or Resume to see what I've been up to!

Photography

I was originally inspired to seriously start photography back in 2011. What started simply as a way to document my summer quickly grew into a love of capturing those fleeting moments in life that ordinarily would just pass us by.

Along my journey, I’ve encountered a lot of conflicting philosophies on just what it means to be a photographer. Some saw photography as art, others saw it as truth. It's made me think a lot about where I stand, but all I really know right now is that I believe in capturing how I see the world. Whether that’s the ‘truth’ or not, well that’s up for debate. But whatever it is, it’s my truth. I never set out to be a photojournalist, and I didn’t set out to be an artist either. All I wanted was to remember the moment, to capture those stories and those emotions and all the countless other things that make up that little piece of history. And that’s still what I want, and it will continue to be what I want. That much I know for sure.

Photography has taken me to amazing places, both literally and figuratively, and I’ll continue to follow it — wherever the clouds take me next.

If you're interested in seeing more of my photography, you can check out my photoblog, Chasing Clouds, or my Portfolio!
