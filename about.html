About Me

Where I've been

Yext

I'm in my second year at Yext now, having been here since July 2018. Originally I was a member of the R&D Team, and for awhile I was the only full-time developer on the team (just me and my manager, it was a wild ride). Those few months were filled with some of the most challenging yet fulfilling experiences I've had at Yext so far. I was able to grow so much as an engineer and an individual during this time and I wouldn't have had it any other way. Eventually, my tiny team got merged into another team. Despite leaving the R&D team, I didn't leave R&D duties behind. There's always something new and exciting happening here, and I'm lucky that I get to be here to contribute to it.

Biogen

The summer of my junior year of college, I interned at Biogen, a biotechnology corporation located in the Boston area. This experience left a deep impression on me and played a large role in shaping my plans for future. It's there that I had the unique opportunity to tackle a problem that's continued to plague even the biggest biotech players out there. Though I won't be so arrogant as to claim that my solution was perfect when seasoned professionals have been pooling their resources towards developing their own solutions, it was complete and more importantly, it worked. Over the course of three months, I had successfully written a program from scratch that could analyze 3D neuronal microscopy scans, the structures that individual voxels corresponded to, compile metrics on the relational structure of the neuron and the density of dendritic spines, and visualize the results in interactive 3D.
